Programmable Music Box

The programmable music box is a project I wrote software for at the Dos Pueblos Engineering academy in my senior year of high school with one other student.

Normal music boxes are built so that each columns of pins can only play one note. This is because the tines that are plucked by each column have a fixed length.

The programmable music box allows users to create their own tune on a music box. The holes in the rotating drum have magnets at their bottom, so people can simply drop the provided metal pins into these holes, set the drum spinning, then listen to the song they’ve created.

Users can also program the “tines” (actually just metal detectors) so that they play different instruments and notes.

The music box was built by other students, but like the self-playing guitar, I programmed the UI, which ran on a Raspberry Pi, and my fellow programmer hooked up a slave Arduino to the metal detectors and synthesizer.
